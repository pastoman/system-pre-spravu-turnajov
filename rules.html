  1. GENERAL RULES
    • These are just GENERAL RULES that apply to all series. Due to the often specific series, each series also has Rules for that series, which are just as binding as this general one. If the General and Specific Rules conflict with each other, the Specific Rules for that series have more weight.
      1. Every rider is obliged to use his real name on the track in the format First Name Surname.
      2. Polite behavior is expected from the participants, both on the web and on the server, as well as on Discord.
      3. Before entering the server, it is everyone's responsibility to make sure that their game is up-to-date, and their internet connection is also up-to-date. Otherwise, he should leave the server so as not to spoil the experience for others.
      4. Unless the general rules specify otherwise - The team consists of a maximum of two drivers - a maximum of two identical cars of the same make, same model, same category. These cars cannot be changed during the season.
  2. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RIDER ON TRACK
      1. Drive fair towards other pilots (don't push, don't block...)
      2. Respect the flags.
      3. Do not violate the track limits (see point 4).
      4. Do not cross the line at the exit from the pits.
  3. CHAMPIONSHIP REGISTRATION
      1. You can register for the championship via the link in the menu of the given championship.
  4. TRACK LIMITS
      1. The track limit is determined by the game, which itself punishes their violation.
      2. A driver who receives a penalty for cuts in the last lap will be moved up the order after the race. After the race, the order will be corrected, the driver will be returned to his position and will be credited with the time that would have cost him to serve the penalty during a normal round. The rider must log into the forum himself immediately after the race, that the game has punished him in this way - later complaints will not be taken into account.
      3. If a driver receives a penalty in the race before the last lap, he is obliged to serve it in the race - rule will be applied only to the penalties that were given in the last round!
  5. QUALIFYING
      1. Qualifying will determine the order of riders at the start.
      2. It is forbidden to chat during the qualifying.
      3. In the approach to the timed lap and in the timed lap, it is not allowed to use the track outside the limits to gain an advantage (see rule 4).
      4. The use of ESC (return to the garage) is TOTALLY prohibited in the qualifying. The driver must drive to the pits, refuel and change tires during the pit. Pressing ESC means the end of qualification. Another trip to the track means the punishment of banning participation in the qualification in the next race!
      5. Cuts in the qualification are controlled by the game and if the player is beyond the limits of the track, the game deletes his time.
  6. RACE
      1. It is strictly forbidden to chat during the race.
      2. To be admitted to the race, the rider must be trained and must not be a danger to those around him with his riding. He must be in control of his car.
      3. Team directing is prohibited.
      4. After finishing the race, it is forbidden to crash into rivals, and it is part of the circuit's accessories and everyone's duty to safely go around the pits.
      5. Continuous flashing is prohibited! Light flashing on the straights is tolerated.
      6. If the car is not seriously damaged, the pit limiter must be driven at the maximum permitted speed in the pit lane.
  7. FLAGS
      1. Yellow flag - slow down and be ready to stop, if the rider does not do so and gains an advantage (passes the opponent) or causes a collision, a penalty will follow.
      2. Blue flag - the rider is obliged to let a faster rider in front of him within three turns at most from the moment the flag is displayed.
  8. PROTESTS
      1. Protests are submitted within 72 hours after the end of the race.
      2. The administrator investigates the exit from the pits and the filed protests.
      3. The administrator can investigate the entire plant of his own volition, but it is not his duty.
  9. PENALTIES
      1. Reprimand. (for 3 reprimands, pit lane drive through will be given)
      2. Addition of X places at the start of the race.
      3. Prohibition of qualifying and start from the last place.
      4. Prohibition of qualifying and start from the pits
      5. Drive through the pit lane or Stop and Go for three laps. (D/T + 10sec and S/G + 22sec to race time)
      6. Disqualification from the race.
      7. Prohibition of start in the next race.
      8. Disqualification from the championship.
      9. Disqualification from the championship.
  10. ON-TRACK MANEUVERS
      1. The rider in front has the right to choose the track. This does not apply if:
        1. The second rider is with a part of his car next to the first.
        2. If the leading driver has already changed the driving direction once.
      2. It is forbidden to push race participants of the track in any form.
      3. A stationary car (either after an accident or its own fault) will wait until the other drivers have passed and will be able to safely turn around/return to the track.
  11. OVERTAKING
      1. The attacking rider (yellow car) is more than half the car next to the defender/opponent (red car).
        The attacker is at the apex with more than half of his car next to the defender (red car). The attacker thus has the right to a racing track, and the collision at the apex is the fault of the defender.
      2. The attacking rider (yellow car) is less than half a car next to the defender/opponent (red car).
        The attacker only has the part of the car near the opponent's rear wheel on the apex. Thus, the defender has the right to a racing track, and the collision on the apex is the attacker's fault.
      3. The attacking rider (yellow car) is roughly half-and-half with the defender/opponent (red car).
        The attacker has his front wheels on the apex before the rear wheels of the defender. Both riders thus have the right to the racing track. If a collision occurs between riders, they are both mutually to blame. In this case, a racing incident may occur in the decision-making process regarding the assessment of guilt.
      4. Side by Side
        If opponents enter a turn lap by lap, next to each other, both must choose to choose such a track to leave the inner or outer track free for their opponent.
